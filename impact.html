Improved Cattle Production

Enhance cattle production and increase the value of livestock products in the region. By addressing key challenges in animal health, feeding, breeding, and market access, farmers can achieve higher productivity and better prices, raising farmer incomes by an estimated $3.3 million each year.

Restore Degraded Rangelands

Restore degraded rangelands focusing on improving pasture quality, increase forage availability, and support more productive and sustainable livestock systems. These improvements contribute to healthier ecosystems, reduced land degradation, and greater resilience to climate shocks.

Expected Outcomes

Disease and Mortality

  • Liver condemnation reduced from 80% to less than 15%
  • Average mortality rates reduced from 10% to 5%

Reproduction and Herd Structure

  • Calving rate increased from 30% to over 60%
  • Market age reduced from 5+ years to under 3 years
  • Average carcass weight increased by 50kg

Grazing and Nutrition

  • Supplementary feeding and fodder banks developed
  • Gut microbiome diversity and digestion efficiency improved
  • Enteric methane emissions intensity reduced
  • Carbon sequestration reversed from net loss to 0.8 tCO‚ÇÇe per hectare per year
  • Increased biodiversity (vegetation and soil microbiome)

Cattle as a Business

  • Annual off-take increased from 8% to over 15%
  • Carcass value increased from ZMW 36/kg to ZMW 54/kg
  • ZMW 6,500 extra income per animal brought to market
  • ZMW 25,000 extra income from beef sales per farmer per year
  • ZMW 355 million extra income per year across full project area

Dual Purpose Genetics

  • Daily lactation increased from 4 to 8 liters per animal
  • Lactation period increased from 90 to 200 days per year
  • ZMW 6,600 from milk sales per farmer per year
  • 2 liters per day can be sold (after calf and household consumption)

Monitoring, Reporting and Verification (MRV)

The BR4RP will employ state-of-the-art environmental monitoring, reporting and verification systems to ensure effective and adaptive management strategies provide maximum environmental benefit to farmers and generate carbon credits with very high environmental integrity.

The development share of the gross carbon revenue from each project instance will cover all development and ongoing MRV costs for that instance, and the development of additional nature-based revenue streams (e.g., emissions intensity certification and biodiversity credits) and project instances.
