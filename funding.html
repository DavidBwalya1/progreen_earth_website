Direct Community Benefit

The budget includes approximately $7.5 million (63% of total) for activities that directly benefit communities over the first 5 years.

Benefit Sharing Mechanism

Based on gross carbon revenue: Barotse Royal Establishment receives 5%, Government of Zambia receives 5%, and the remaining 90% is reinvested in the project and communities.

High Integrity Net Carbon Removals

Social Integrity

Regenerative agriculture carbon removals offer an advantage over conservation or reforestation, as carbon is sequestered through a non-competing land use that simultaneously enhances food production. The co-design and development strategy ensures social safeguarding, efficient implementation, and equitable benefit sharing.

Environmental Integrity

The same activities that generate carbon removals simultaneously create significant socio-economic benefits, increasing the likelihood of long-term practice adoption and carbon sequestration permanence. Leakage risk is minimal, as these activities enhance rather than displace existing livelihoods.

Development Timeline

Initial instance (200,000ha) development timeline to first carbon credit issuance

The project follows a structured development approach to ensure financial self-sustainability through ongoing VCU sales. Livestock production co-benefit costs equate to roughly half the total budget, making the project highly suitable for development finance.
